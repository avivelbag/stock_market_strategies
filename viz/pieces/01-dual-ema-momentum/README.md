# Dual EMA Crossover Momentum — visualised

An interactive, self-explaining backtest of the momentum-premium strategy in
`strategies/01-dual-ema-momentum/`.

## What it teaches

Momentum works *because trends persist* — and fails the moment they don't. The
page runs the unchanged rule on four synthetic regimes and lets you flip
between them:

- **trend_gbm** — a clean uptrend; the crossover should be in its element.
- **mean_rev_ou** — oscillation around 100; every crossover is a trap.
- **regime_switch** — alternating trend/chop; the realistic middle ground.
- **fat_tail** — zero drift, violent jumps; the EMA filter is too slow.

The point is the *contrast*: the same logic, no re-tuning, opposite outcomes.

## What you see

1. **Price, signal & trades** — close price with `EMA(20)` and `EMA(60)`
   overlaid, the held-long periods shaded, and ▲/▼ entry/exit markers.
2. **Strategy equity vs buy & hold** — net-of-cost equity against the dashed
   benchmark, both starting at ×1.00.
3. **Metrics** — in-sample Sharpe/CAGR/drawdown next to the walk-forward
   out-of-sample tiles. The gap between them is the lesson in overfitting.
4. **Interpretation** — a computed, honest sentence: did the result match the
   thesis, and were the out-of-sample folds actually profitable?
