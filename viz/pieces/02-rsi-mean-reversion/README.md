# RSI Mean-Reversion (Connors RSI-2) — visualised

An interactive backtest of the behavioural-reversal strategy in
`strategies/02-rsi-mean-reversion/`.

## What it teaches

This is the structural opposite of momentum: it *buys panic*. When a 2-day RSI
collapses below 10 the crowd has overreacted to the downside; the strategy goes
long and waits for the snap-back, exiting when RSI clears 90.

Flip between the four regimes and watch where overreaction-and-revert is a real
edge (mean reversion, fat tails, the chop half of regime_switch) versus where
"buying the dip" just keeps catching a falling knife (a sustained trend).

## What you see

1. **Price, signal & trades** — close price, held-long shading, ▲/▼ markers.
2. **RSI(2) panel** — the exact indicator the rule reads, with the oversold
   (enter) and overbought (exit) threshold lines drawn in.
3. **Strategy equity vs buy & hold** — net-of-cost, both from ×1.00.
4. **Metrics + interpretation** — in-sample vs walk-forward, plus an honest
   computed read on whether the regime suited the thesis.
